How it works

Three steps to speech

1

Point camera at your mouth

VoiceBox uses your webcam to track lip movements in real-time using MediaPipe face detection.

2

AI reads your lips

The Chaplin visual speech recognition model predicts words from mouth motion. Faster-Whisper handles any audible audio.

3

Hear your words spoken

Tap any word to correct it. Tap the sentence to have it spoken aloud using your device's text-to-speech.

Features

Built for accessibility

🔒

Fully private

All processing runs on your computer. No video, audio, or speech data ever leaves your device.

🎙️

Two modes

Dysphonia: fuses lip-reading + microphone for partial voice. Aphonia: lip-reading only, no audio needed.

📝

Editable predictions

Tap any word to fix it. VoiceBox learns from your corrections and suggests them next time.

⚡

Works offline

No internet connection required. The AI models are bundled inside the installer.

🧠

AI-powered fusion

Chaplin VSR model + Whisper transcription are combined by a fusion engine for higher accuracy.

📱

Quick phrases

Tap common phrases instantly without scanning — great for fast everyday communication.

Installation

Up and running in minutes

1

Download VoiceBox-Setup-x64.exe from the button above.

2

Double-click the installer. If Windows SmartScreen appears, click More info → Run anyway (the app is not yet code-signed).

3

Follow the setup wizard. VoiceBox installs to C:\Program Files\VoiceBox\ by default.

4

Launch VoiceBox from the Start menu or Desktop shortcut. Allow camera access when prompted.

1

Download VoiceBox.dmg from the button above.

2

Open the .dmg file and drag VoiceBox.app into your Applications folder.

3

First launch: right-click VoiceBox in Applications → Open → Open (bypasses Gatekeeper for unsigned apps).

4

Grant camera and microphone access in System Settings → Privacy & Security when prompted.

1

Download VoiceBox-x86_64.AppImage from the button above.

2

Make it executable: chmod +x VoiceBox-x86_64.AppImage

3

Run it: ./VoiceBox-x86_64.AppImage — or double-click in your file manager.

4

If the camera isn't detected, add yourself to the video group: sudo usermod -aG video $USER then log out and back in.
